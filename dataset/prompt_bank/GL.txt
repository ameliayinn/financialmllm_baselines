Fitch Ratings-New York-05 November 2020:

Fitch Ratings has affirmed the Insurer Financial Strength (IFS) Ratings of Globe Life Inc . and its operating subsidiaries, which include Globe Life & Accident Insurance Co ., American Income Life Insurance Co ., United American Insurance Co ., and Liberty National Life Insurance Co . at 'A+'. Fitch has also affirmed Globe Life Inc .'s Long-Term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) at 'A-'. The Rating Outlook is Stable.

Key Rating Drivers

The rating affirmation reflects Globe Life's very strong and stable operating profitability, conservative operating strategy, strong capital position, consistent debt servicing capability and above-average reserve margins. Fitch views the impact of the economic fallout as material, but manageable for Globe Life, given its balance sheet composition. The Stable Outlook reflects Fitch's expectation that the company will continue to maintain its very strong and above-average financial returns.

Fitch views Globe Life's business profile as very strong, which considers its below-average risk profile and the lack of complexity and market risk in its liabilities. The company's strategic focus is on the sale of life and supplemental health insurance products to the middle-income market through controlled distribution, which drives its consistent and strong earnings. The company's diversification trails peers, but Globe Life lacks exposure to risky products, including long-term care insurance and variable annuities with living benefit guarantees. Favorably, Globe Life has a diversified distribution model and firmly entrenched market position.

Globe Life's financial performance and earnings remain strong. Through 9M20, the company reported a ROE of 13.6%, which exceeds expectations for the current rating level. YTD, strong and improved health results offset deterioration in the life segment due to increased mortality stemming from COVID-19. Globe Life's strong earnings are supported by below-average exposure to market and interest sensitive liabilities. Fitch expects the company will continue to generate above-average returns due to its product profile and predictable earnings streams. The uptick in mortality associated with COVID-19 is expected to remain manageable.

Fitch views Globe Life's capital position as strong, which considers its 'Strong' Prism score at YE 2019. The quality of capital is considered below-average, reflecting the high proportion of intercompany holdings. The company's estimated RBC ratio, solely accounting for YTD downgrades and credit losses, was 310% as of 9M20, which is the mid-point of its target range of 300%-320%. Financial leverage is in line with rating expectations at 26%, which includes the company's issuance of $400 million in senior notes in 3Q20.

Debt-service capability remains strong with Globe Life's fixed-charge coverage ratio of 12x based on Fitch's calculations through 9M20. Financial flexibility is considered strong, benefitting from the company's diversified funding sources and backup liquidity. Holding company liquidity is strong with liquid assets totaling $435 million as of 9M20.

Globe Life's investment portfolio is below-average in risk compared with the industry, reflecting its underweight positions in structured securities, commercial mortgage loans and private equities. The portfolio is concentrated in corporate bonds, which comprise approximately 80% of invested assets. Given this concentration, the portfolio is overweight in NAIC 2 bonds, which represent 55% of total bonds. As such, the portfolio is vulnerable to rating migration in the current environment. Fitch expects material investment losses for Globe Life over the next one to two years, but for rating downgrades to have a more meaningful impact on the portfolio.

Fitch considers Globe Life's interest rate risk as below-average compared with the industry, as its liabilities are largely illiquid, long-dated individual life insurance. The company's annuity exposure, and consequently, its disintermediation risk, are viewed as below-average. Globe Life's reserve margins are very strong and above-average, demonstrated by the company's cashflow testing results, which are positive, robust and consistent across the deterministic interest rate scenarios.

RATING SENSITIVITIES

The ratings remain sensitive to any material change in Fitch's rating case assumptions with respect to the coronavirus pandemic. Periodic updates to Fitch's assumptions are possible given the rapid pace of changes in government actions in response to the pandemic, and the pace with which new information is available on the medical aspects of the outbreak.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Negative Rating Action/Downgrade:

--A material adverse change in Fitch's rating assumptions with respect to the impact of the coronavirus pandemic;

--Weakened financial performance, caused by outsized investment losses or poor underwriting experience, resulting in sustained GAAP based ROE metrics consistently below 10% or ROA metrics below 2%;

--Sustained deterioration in the company's capitalization that would result in a Prism capital model assessment falling to 'Adequate', increased affiliated investments activity, as well as an RBC ratio consistently below 290%;

--Financial leverage metrics consistently above 30%, along with a total financing and commitments (TFC) ratio above 0.5x and fixed-charge coverage metrics consistently below 6.0x.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Positive Rating Action/Upgrade:

--A material positive change in Fitch's ratings assumptions with respect to the impact of the coronavirus;

--A positive rating action prefaced by Fitch's ability to reliably forecast the impact of the coronavirus pandemic on the financial profile of both the North American life insurance industry and Globe Life;

--Sustained improvement in Fitch's assessment of capitalization, as defined by a Prism capital model output well into 'Strong', together with the maintenance of strong statutory reserving methodologies;

--Financial leverage metrics consistently below 25% with a TFC ratio below 0.40x;

--Continued growth across core markets, while maintaining GAAP based ROE metrics consistently in excess of 13% and fixed-charge coverage in excess of 10x.

Best/Worst Case Rating Scenario

International scale credit ratings of Financial Institutions and Covered Bond issuers have a best-case rating upgrade scenario (defined as the 99th percentile of rating transitions, measured in a positive direction) of three notches over a three-year rating horizon; and a worst-case rating downgrade scenario (defined as the 99th percentile of rating transitions, measured in a negative direction) of four notches over three years. The complete span of best- and worst-case scenario credit ratings for all rating categories ranges from 'AAA' to 'D'. Best- and worst-case scenario credit ratings are based on historical performance.
