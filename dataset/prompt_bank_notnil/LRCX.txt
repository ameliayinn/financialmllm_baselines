General Electric gained 55 cents, or 4.3%, to $13.44, while the S&P 500 fell 45.93 points, or 1.73%, to 2612.62.

The troubled industrial giant had its best day in years on speculation that Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B) could buy a stake in the company, as Buffett has said previously that he might consider an investment if the price were right.

Although no one's confirmed anything, someone's buying GE, which also has an unexpected cloud-computing angle.

GE is down 23% year-to-date for a 54.8% collapse in the past year.

The Biggest Loser

Nvidia (NVDA) slid to the bottom of the S&P 5oo on Tuesday, as it suspended self-driving car tests.

Nvidia reversed earlier gains to close lower after saying it would halt testing of autonomous cars, a week after a pedestrian was struck and killed by an Uber car in Arizona.

Nvidia is up 16.6% year-to-date, and has surged 109.8% in the past 12 months.
