GE shares fell 0.3% premarket after The Wall Street Journal reported that a housecleaning at the industrial conglomerate's board will remove many long-term associates of former Chief Executive Jeff Immelt and aims to create a board that is more closely aligned with CEO John Flannery's vision.

Shares of Verizon Communications rose 1% before the opening bell following a Wells Fargo analyst upgrade to outperform from market perform. Wells Fargo also raised its price target on shares of the wireless carrier to $50 from $48. The stock closed at $45.42 Friday. Additionally, Bloomberg reported that the firm is close to a new deal with the National Football League for digital streaming rights of game broadcasts.

PayPal Holdings shares were up 0.8% premarket. The payments giant said it is connecting its website and smartphone apps with those of Acorns Grow Inc., a five-year-old automated savings and investment service, in a move into robo-investing.

Shares of Wal-Mart Stores declined 1.1% premarket after e-commerce giant Alibaba said it will pay $2.88 billion for a 36% stake in China's second-largest big-box retailer, Sun Art Retail Group, one of Wal-Mart's rivals in the country. Additionally, Goldman Sachs downgraded Wal-Mart to neutral from buy.

Blue Apron Holdings Inc. shares rose 1% premarket after KeyBanc Capital Markets initiated coverage on the meal kit delivery firm with a sector perform rating.

Shares of Amazon.com Inc. climbed 0.2% before the opening bell following an Instinet price target increase to $1,360, the highest of roughly 45 analysts tracked by FactSet. Shares closed at $1,129.88 Friday and would need to gain 20% to hit Instinet's price target.

Qualcomm Inc. shares advanced 0.5% premarket after Reuters reported that the chip maker is set to win Japanese antitrust clearance for its $38-billion bid for NXP Semiconductors and gain Europe's approval by the end of the year.

Time Warner Inc. shares were little changed premarket. "Justice League," a Warner Bros.' megabudget production, opened to an estimated $96 million in the U.S. and Canada this weekend. The Time Warner Inc.-owned studio spent nearly $300 million to make the movie. Though not a flop, that is well below the studio's hopes and less than any DC superhero movie since the 2011 flop "Green Lantern."

Shares of Acorda Therapeutics Inc. fell 1.7% before the opening bell after the firm said it is discontinuing studies of its Parkinson's disease treatment tozadenant.

Cardinal Health Inc. shares declined 3.7% premarket following a Morgan Stanley analyst downgrade to underweight from equal weight.
