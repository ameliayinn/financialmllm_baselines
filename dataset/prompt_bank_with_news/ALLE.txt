Fitch Ratings -Toronto-16 November 2021:

Fitch Ratings has assigned a 'BBB' rating to Leggett & Platt, Incorporated 's (Leggett) new offering of US$500 million senior unsecured notes due 2051. Leggett intends to use net proceeds from this issuance to refinance commercial paper. The transaction is leverage neutral, and Fitch views it as credit neutral.

This issuance follows Leggett's refinancing of its revolving credit facility during 3Q'21, which extended maturity from January 2024 to September 2026. Additionally, Leggett fully repaid its remaining $280 million Term Loan A during Q3 2021. Pro forma for the transaction, the company's liquidity resources will include $235 million in cash and an undrawn $1.2 billion revolver. Fitch expects Leggett to refinance its $300 million bond maturing August 2022 on a leverage-neutral basis.

Key Rating Drivers

Recovery Underway: Following the onset of the pandemic in 2020, Leggett took several steps to preserve cash and margins which included cost-cutting and deferring/delaying capex. These measures, combined with a pronounced rebound in end markets, resulted in stronger than expected revenue performance through the second half of 2020 which has continued into 2021. The pricing environment has also contributed to EBITDA and FCF margin growth.

Management has observed a re-allocation of household spending, as consumers forego vacations and other discretionary expenditures. Fitch expects a partial reversal of this trend, together with cost pressure on the raw material and labor input side, to be a moderate weight on margins during 2021.

Resumption of Deleveraging Path: Debt taken on to fund the 2019 Elite Comfort Solutions, Inc. (ECS) acquisition drove total debt/EBITDA leverage from 2.0x at YE 2018 to 3.0x at YE 2019, which exceeded the company's public leverage target of 2.5x. The pandemic delayed the company's plan to materially delever during 2020. As Leggett's end markets continue to recover, Fitch expects the company to continue its deleveraging path through a combination of EBITDA growth and debt repayments.

Bedding Industry Disruption: The company's core bedding business is shifting to accommodate online purchasing and delivery of compressed mattresses, which the company believes could account for half of the U.S. market by 2026. Leggett is well positioned to capitalize on this trend following its acquisition of ECS, which gives it improved capabilities to develop and manufacture full-foam and hybrid components and finished mattresses. Fitch expects the company will adjust its portfolio of business over time to leverage its competitive advantages in customer engagement, co-design and flexible manufacturing.

Diverse End Markets: Fitch views the company's focus on several different end markets to be a credit positive, reducing cyclicality as well as reliance on any one customer. In addition to its exposure to the bedding market, Leggett has a sizable automotive business that benefits from several long-running trends (increased vehicle size, increased content and consumer preference for comfort), which Fitch expects will drive growth at a faster pace than the automotive market overall. Fitch expects the flooring business to remain steady over time, and the furniture segment provides added diversity. In addition, the company has invested in new businesses (including aerospace and hydraulics), which further diversifies its end-market exposure.

Bedding Consumer Behavior: U.S. bedding sales largely track GDP. Since two-thirds of bedding and furniture purchases are replacement items, consumer sentiment is a stronger leading indicator than real estate trends, such as new housing starts. The U.S. consumer confidence index bottomed out in June 2020 at around 98.5, higher than previous downturns (96.8 in 2011 and 96.6 in 2009), and currently sits around 100.0. Mattresses are deferrable large ticket purchases with an average replacement cycle of 8-15 years.

Co-Design Expertise; Low-Cost Manufacturing: The company benefits from its co-design relationships with manufacturers, as well as scale in fragmented industries. Product development is a core competency, with many of its furniture, bedding and automotive components being co-designed. Leggett has over 1,500 patents. The company's manufacturing is vertically integrated, as it sources scrap steel to produce steel rod and wire in its own mill. This reduces both cost and supply risk. Overall, steel accounts for approximately 25% of raw materials and chemicals account for approximately 15%, and price increases for these inputs are typically passed on to customers with a 60- to 90-day lag.

Consistent FCF Generation: The company generated consistently positive FCF during the past few years, with FCF margins in the low-to-mid single digit range since 2016. Fitch forecasts the company to generate FCF margins of around 3%-4% in 2021 and beyond, which we expect to be allocated towards ECS-acquisition debt repayment, modest dividend increases and limited bolt-on M&A. Fitch expects the company will refrain from larger acquisitions and share repurchases as it focuses on deleveraging over the short to medium term.

Derivation Summary

Leggett serves a much broader range of end markets than other companies in its peer set. Fitch considers the closest comparables to be from the building material and diversified manufacturing spaces. Leggett compares to Masco Corporation (BBB/Stable), which is similarly sized, exposed to less diverse and less stable end markets, and carries slightly lower leverage.

Compared to Allegion plc (BBB-/Positive), Leggett is larger and features superior diversification, though its margins are lower. Vontier Corporation (BBB-/Stable) is smaller than Leggett with less end-market diversity and similar leverage. Compared to Mohawk Industries, Inc . (BBB+/Stable) Leggett is smaller, with similar margins and higher leverage, though Leggett does benefit from superior end-market diversity. IDEX Corporation (BBB+/Stable) carries lower leverage and generates greater margins, though it is smaller and less diversified than Leggett.

After accounting for the ECS acquisition and its effect on leverage, Leggett has shown greater business stability than peers. The company's liquidity and profitability metrics are quite good for the rating category, with its strong financial profile partly offset by elevated leverage, which Fitch expects to moderate over the near to medium term.

Key Assumptions

Fitch's Key Assumptions Within Our Rating Case for the Issuer

Fitch forecasts a 16% rebound in revenue in 2021, reflecting a combination of volume growth in the mid- to high-single-digit range, and price increases driven by strong consumer demand and raw material cost inflation pass-through. Revenue growth assumptions in the mid-single-digit range in 2022 and beyond reflects the long-run average growth for Leggett's key end markets.

Reflecting higher operating costs (mostly raw materials), which are partly passed on to the consumer, Fitch projects EBITDA margin pressure through 2021 followed by a gradual recovery, as cost-cutting efforts balance inflationary pressure.

Leggett reduced capex by 60% to $66 million in 2020 through cost-cutting efforts. In order to catch up on deferred maintenance and roll-out growth initiatives, Fitch forecasts capex to rebound in line with the long-term average of 3.0%-3.5% of revenue.

The company has resumed minor bolt-on acquisitions, which Fitch expects to continue at a limited level through the forecast period. The company fully prepaid its term loan A in August 2021. Fitch expects the company to refinance its 2022 maturity on a leverage-neutral basis. No share repurchases have been assumed by Fitch in the short to medium term, and Fitch expects the company to continue pursuing incremental dividend increases.

Debt/EBITDA leverage returns to the mid-2.0x range around during 2022.

RATING SENSITIVITIES

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Positive Rating Action/Upgrade

--Given the additional debt and higher leverage taken on to fund the ECS acquisition, as well as the impact of the pandemic on the company's operations, we do not envision an upgrade in the near term;

--In the long term, a sustained increase in FCF margin to over 5% could lead to positive rating action;

--A more conservative financial policy leading to a sustained decrease in FFO leverage to below 2.75x, or a sustained decrease in total debt/EBITDA leverage below 2.0x, could also position the company for an upgrade.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Negative Rating Action/Downgrade

--A loss in competitiveness in one or more a key operating segments, leading to FCF margins falling below 2%;

--FFO leverage remains above 3.25x, or total debt/EBITDA leverage remains above 2.5x on a sustained basis.

Liquidity and Debt Structure

Strong Liquidity: Leggett's liquidity position is strong for the rating category, with a Sept. 30, 2021 cash balance of $235 million and $1.2 billion available under its revolver proforma for the November 2021 bond issuance. The company generally keeps a cash balance between $200 million and $300 million. Liquidity is also supported by positive FCF.

The company's senior unsecured notes rank pari passu with its $1.2 billion senior unsecured revolving credit facility, which functions as a CP backstop. The company's debt maturity profile is generally well staggered. The next material maturity a $300 million note due August 2022. Fitch expects this to be refinanced on a leverage-neutral basis. During the third quarter of 2021, the company fully repaid its remaining $280 million term loan A balance, and extended the maturity date of its revolving credit facility from January 2024 to September 2026.

Leggett & Platt, Incorporated is a manufacturer of finished products and critical components for the bedding, automotive, furniture, and flooring end markets.
